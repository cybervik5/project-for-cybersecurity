How Deauthentication Attacks Work

Deauthentication attacks exploit the unprotected management frames in the 802.11 WiFi protocol:

Technical Process:

  1. Attacker identifies target devices and their access point
  2. Attacker forges deauthentication frames spoofing the AP
  3. Frames are sent to client devices at high rate
  4. Devices disconnect believing the request came from AP
  5. Devices attempt to reconnect automatically

Protection Methods:

  • Use WPA3 encryption with Protected Management Frames
  • Implement 802.11w (management frame protection)
  • Monitor for suspicious deauthentication frames
  • Use enterprise authentication (802.1X)

Ethical Considerations

This simulation is for educational purposes only. Performing deauthentication attacks on networks you don't own or have permission to test is illegal in most jurisdictions. Always obtain written permission before conducting any security testing.
